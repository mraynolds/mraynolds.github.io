Nashville PD Traffic Stops: A Statistical Analysis of Bias

Analyzed 3 million+ traffic stop records (2010–2018) using statistical methods in R to identify racial disparities in stops, searches, and arrests. Black and Hispanic drivers were stopped, searched, and arrested at significantly higher rates than white drivers.

Politicians and Donors in 2024: A Bipartite Network Analysis

Constructed a bipartite network of federal candidates and top donors to their principal campaign committees using FEC API data. Identified asymmetric donation patterns between parties and found that the highest fundraising was concentrated around leadership and candidates in competitive races.

Python Networks API

Centrality Within The 118th US Congress

Built a cosponsorship network of US House members from the 118th Congress using congressional API data and analyzed degree, betweenness, and eigenvector centrality. Identified a core group of ubiquitous cosponsors and found that minority party members structurally position themselves adjacent to majority power brokers to maintain legislative relevance.

Python Networks API

Sentiment Analysis of Presidential Addresses to Congress in Election Years

Scraped and analyzed the text of Presidential Addresses to Congress from 1947 to 2022 using sentiment analysis in R. Found no significant correlation between speech sentiment and presidential approval ratings or the partisan composition of Congress.

Predicting Plant Stress with Machine Learning

Compared regression, KNN, and SVM models to classify plant stress conditions using a structured sensor dataset. KNN achieved 98% precision; analysis identified soil moisture and nitrogen level as the two primary drivers of stress.
